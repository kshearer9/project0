Light

Bright but indirect sunlight is ideal for a Ficus Ginseng so keep it on the windowsill, although be careful to not let it get too draughty.

Watering

Water Generously when the soil dries. This should be around once a week usually, but less in Winter.

Pruning & Cleaning

To keep your bonsai small, pruning is required. Snip new shoots off as they appear to retain the tree’s shape. Pruning your bonsai regularly will trigger it to branch out more and foliage will grow more dense and beautiful but make sure to only prune during periods of active growth. As a rough guide, prune back to 2 leaves after 6-8 have grown. If the leaves of your bonsai tree get dusty, they can block sunlight which will stunt growth, so wipe the leaves often with a damp cloth.

Humidity

Ficus ginseng bonsais love humid climates so mist the leaves with water every day if possible. If you want your bonsai to grow aerial roots that will reach the ground, you must do this to keep the air constantly moist.

Fertilising your Bonsai

Fertilise every 1 or 2 weeks in summer and 2 to 4 weeks in winter (if the growth doesn’t stop). Liquid fertiliser or pellets can be used.

Repotting
Repot every 2 years in Spring, using a bonsai soil.
